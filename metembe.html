About Our School

St. John’s Metembe Secondary School traces its roots back to 1974, when it was established through the efforts of the late Dr. Zachary Onyonka, then the Member of Parliament for Kitutu Chache. The school was first founded as a boarding institution for girls under the name Metembe Girls, and it played a significant role in expanding access to secondary education in the region. Shortly afterward, sponsorship was taken over by the Catholic Church, which provided stability and guidance in its formative years. It was at this point that the school adopted its current name, St. John’s Metembe Secondary School, marking the beginning of its long journey as an institution of learning. The early years of the school were marked by both promise and challenges. During the late 1990s, particularly between 1997 and 1999, the school experienced its highest enrolment, reaching close to 400 students. This period represented the peak of its student population. However, the rapid growth of nearby secondary schools such as Nyansakia, Nyakeyo, Omogumo, Tambacha, and Nyakoora gradually reduced the catchment area, leading to a noticeable decline in numbers. The competition for students became a defining factor in the decades that followed, reshaping the fortunes of St. John’s Metembe. The year 1979 stands out as one of the most difficult moments in the school’s history. Enrolment dropped sharply to the point where closure seemed almost inevitable. It was only through the intervention of the District Education Office, which engaged the local community and emphasized the importance of education, that the school remained open. Government support in the form of grants also played a crucial role in sustaining operations, though the issue of levies and community contributions remained a recurring challenge. Despite these obstacles, the school managed to remain afloat, a testimony to the resilience of both its leadership and the surrounding community. Leadership at St. John’s Metembe has passed through the hands of a number of principals, each contributing to the institution in their own time. The first Teachers Service Commission–appointed principal was Mr. Nyambeche, who laid the foundation for administration and academic structure. He was succeeded by Mr. Nyaseti, under whose tenure the school continued to build its systems. In 1996, Mr. Pingo assumed office, working with a staff of five T.S.C. teachers to strengthen the academic program. His tenure was followed in 2004 by Mr. Erick Onsongo, who in turn was succeeded in 2006 by Mr. Patric Otwori, serving until 2011. Afterward, Mr. Zachary Nyagoto took over, guiding the school from 2011 until his departure in 2018. The next phase of leadership began in 2019, when Mr. Christopher Ayieko K. became principal. His tenure lasted until 2021, during which time the school’s enrolment rose to about 278 students, reflecting renewed growth in student numbers. In 2021, leadership was handed over to Mr. Kenneth Ogato, who served until January 2025. At the start of 2025, Mr. Evans Mogere assumed office and is the current principal. Today, St. John’s Metembe stands at a transitional point. The school has an enrolment of 118 students, organized into three classes: Form 2, Form 3, and Form 4. In line with the national transition from the 8-4-4 system to the Competency-Based Education (CBE) curriculum, the Form 1 class was not admitted, and the traditional intake has been phased out. This shift marks both a challenge and an opportunity, as the school adapts to changing educational policies while continuing to serve the local community.
