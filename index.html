About Our School

St. John's Metembe Secondary School was established in 1974 through the efforts of the late Dr. Zachary Onyonka, then the Member of Parliament for Kitutu Chache. Originally founded as a boarding institution for girls named Metembe Girls, the school was later taken over by the Catholic Church and renamed St. John's Metembe Secondary School.

Throughout its history, the school has experienced both growth and challenges. Today, St. John's Metembe continues to serve the local community with a focus on providing quality education and developing well-rounded individuals.

The school has been led by several principals over the years, each contributing to its development. The first TSC-appointed principal was Mr. Nyambeche, followed by Mr. Nyaseti, Mr. Pingo (1996), Mr. Erick Onsongo (2004), Mr. Patric Otwori (2006-2011), Mr. Zachary Nyagoto (2011-2018), Mr. Christopher Ayieko K. (2019-2021), Mr. Kenneth Ogato (2021-2025), and currently Mr. Evans Mogere (2025-present).

Today, St. John's Metembe has an enrollment of 118 students across Form 2, Form 3, and Form 4 classes. In line with the national transition to the Competency-Based Education (CBE) curriculum, the school did not admit a Form 1 class, marking a significant shift in its educational approach while continuing to serve the local community.
